A long-form essay. One main idea, explored from multiple angles. 1000-2000 words is the sweet spot.

## Opening

[Hook the reader with a scene, question, or surprising fact. Make them want the next paragraph.]

## Thesis

[State the main idea in one or two sentences. Be specific. The rest of the essay earns this.]

## Body 1: [Sub-argument]

[One or two paragraphs. Evidence, examples, story. Make the reader feel it before you argue it.]

## Body 2: [Sub-argument]

[One or two paragraphs. Push back on the obvious reading.]

## Body 3: [Sub-argument]

[One or two paragraphs. The strongest case in your own words.]

## Counter

[What would a smart skeptic say? Address it honestly. This is where trust is built.]

## Closing

[Return to the opening. Restate the thesis in light of what you just argued. Leave the reader with something to think about — not a call to action, not a moral.]
